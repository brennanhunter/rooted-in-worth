<p class="intro-line">An intergenerational, trauma-informed community built around permanency for foster youth. A real-world proof of concept for what we're building.</p>

<div class="section">
  <div class="section-label">Who They Are</div>
  <h2>The Basics</h2>
  <p>Bridge Meadows is an Oregon-based nonprofit that creates <strong>intergenerational intentional communities</strong> centered on children and youth who have experienced foster care. Founded in 2005, it now operates four communities across Oregon and is expanding into Washington state.</p>
  <p>Each community brings together three generations under one roof: <strong>adoptive and kinship families</strong> raising children formerly in foster care, <strong>elders 55+</strong> who serve as mentors and neighbors, and <strong>young adults ages 17–24</strong> who have aged out of the system themselves. It is not a program. It is a place where people live.</p>
</div>

<div class="section">
  <div class="section-label">How It Works</div>
  <h2>Their Three-Part Recipe</h2>
  <div class="three-pillars">
    <div class="pillar">
      <div class="pillar-icon">🏡</div>
      <h3>Affordable Housing</h3>
      <p>Intentionally designed spaces that encourage connection while providing safety, privacy, and stability for all three generations.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pillar">
      <div class="pillar-icon">🌿</div>
      <h3>Genuine Connection</h3>
      <p>Shared meals, community gardens, evening check-ins, and intergenerational activities that build real relationship over time.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="pillar">
      <div class="pillar-icon">💛</div>
      <h3>Clinical Support</h3>
      <p>Onsite trauma-informed clinical staff use strengths-based approaches to weave the community together and support healing.</p>

  <p>Their architecture is even designed with trauma in mind. Courtyards face inward. Common areas invite spontaneous contact. Therapy rooms are embedded in the building, not across town.</p>
